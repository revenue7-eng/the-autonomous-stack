# Glossary

Key terms used in The Autonomous Stack.

---

## A

**Autonomy Level**  
A scale (A0–A3) measuring operational independence from cloud and internet.  
- **A0** – Cloud‑bound (requires constant internet).  
- **A1** – Online‑dependent (needs occasional external services).  
- **A2** – Offline‑capable (works without internet but may lack full control).  
- **A3** – Fully autonomous (offline‑first, local data, full pause/exit/recoverability).

---

## E

**Exit**  
The ability to leave a system without losing your data. One of the three ethical criteria from whose.world.

---

## P

**Pause**  
The ability to stop a system at any moment. One of the three ethical criteria.

---

## R

**Recoverability**  
The ability to restore a system to a previous state. One of the three ethical criteria.

---

## T

**Transparency Level**  
A scale (T0–T2) measuring architectural openness.  
- **T0** – Closed (no public documentation or source).  
- **T1** – Documented (architecture described, but source may be closed).  
- **T2** – Open‑source (source code available under OSI license).

---

## V

**Visibility**  
The ability to inspect how a system works (related to Transparency Level). One of the criteria in whose.world.

---

## W

**whose.world**  
The philosophical framework that underpins TAS. It asks: *Whose flow are you in?* and provides the ethical criteria of Pause, Exit, Recoverability, and Visibility.

---

*This glossary will be expanded as new concepts are added.*
